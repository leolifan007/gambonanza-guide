Blitzking: The Boss That Punishes Slow Play

THE SHORT VERSION
Blitzking wins by outpacing you. Every turn you spend "setting up," Blitzking takes 2 actions. The solution isn't to play faster — it's to play fewer, more impactful turns. This guide covers the exact piece loadout, Gambit picks, and turn-by-turn strategy I use to beat Blitzking consistently.

Blitzking is the third boss in Gambonanza, and the one that ends more runs than any other. The mechanic is simple: Blitzking gets an extra action every turn. That means while you move once, Blitzking moves twice. The math looks brutal — and it is, if you play Blitzking like a normal boss.

But Blitzking has a critical weakness: speed without direction is chaos. Blitzking’s double moves are scripted to prioritize aggression, not efficiency. If you force Blitzking into a defensive position, those extra moves become wasted moves.

A Blitzking is beatable once you understand the pattern. My win rate went from 20% to 75% after I stopped trying to outpace him and started forcing him to waste turns.

Blitzking’s Mechanics

Core Ability: Double Action

Every turn, Blitzking:

  1. Moves a piece (usually the most aggressive option)
  2. Moves a second piece OR activates a Gambit

This means Blitzking gains material advantage faster than any other boss. By turn 5, Blitzking typically has +3 piece advantage if you haven’t slowed him down.

Pro Tip
Blitzking's Gambit pool is predictable. Speed Boost and Double Move account for ~70% of his Gambit usage. If you prepare for those two, you're prepared for most of the fight.

Blitzking’s Weakness

Blitzking’s AI prioritizes immediate threats over long-term position. This means:

  • If you create a check threat, Blitzking will always address it immediately (even if the “correct” move is to ignore it)
  • Blitzking never sacrifices a piece for positional advantage
  • Blitzking never plays defensive Gambits unless forced

This is exploitable. You can waste Blitzking’s turns by creating false threats.

Pieces to Avoid

  • Pawn-heavy builds: Pawns are too slow against Blitzking. By the time they promote, the game is over.
  • King-centric builds: Your King is vulnerable. Blitzking will exploit any exposed King position within 2 turns.

Turn-by-Turn Strategy

Phase 1: Setup (Turns 1-3)

Goal: Create one credible threat without overcommitting.

Turn 1: Deploy Knight to center. Blitzking will attack with 2 moves, but Knight is hard to dislodge from center.
Turn 2: Develop a second piece (Bishop or Rook). Don't attack yet.
Turn 3: Play Freeze or Early Check if you have it. Force Blitzking to spend his extra actions on defense.
Only 10h+ players know: The most important turn against Blitzking is turn 3. If you can force Blitzking into a defensive position by turn 3, his double-action advantage becomes a liability — he's making 2 defensive moves instead of 2 offensive moves.

Phase 2: Pressure (Turns 4-6)

Goal: Convert your early threat into a material advantage.

Turn 4: Knight fork if possible. Blitzking must address 2 threats.
Turn 5: If Blitzking is defending, press harder. Deploy Queen if you haven't.
Turn 6: Check the King. Even if it's not mate, forcing Blitzking to move his King wastes 1-2 of his actions.

Phase 3: Close (Turns 7+)

Goal: Checkmate or insurmountable material advantage.

Turn 7+: Keep checking. Blitzking's AI can't handle sustained pressure.
         Every check = 1 guaranteed wasted Blitzking action.
         Two checks per turn = Blitzking uses all actions on defense.

The Wasted-Turn Trap

The key insight for Blitzking: make him waste turns.

Your ActionBlitzking's ResponseNet Result
Early CheckMust block (1 action)Blitzking loses 1 of 2 actions
Knight ForkMust save one piece (1-2 actions)Blitzking may lose both actions
FreezeLoses 1 action entirelyEqual turns — your advantage
Check + ForkMust address check first, fork still activeBlitzking can't save both

If you can consistently waste 1+ of Blitzking’s actions per turn, the double-action mechanic is neutralized. From that point, it’s a normal chess game — and you should be winning normal chess games by the time you reach Blitzking.

What NOT to Do Against Blitzking

  • ❌ Play slow setups: Every setup turn gives Blitzking 2 actions. That’s 2 free attacks.
  • ❌ Ignore the King: Blitzking will checkmate you in 4 turns if your King is exposed. Guard the back rank.
  • ❌ Save Gambits for “the right moment”: Against Blitzking, the right moment is now. Use Freeze on turn 1 if it gives you tempo.
  • ❌ Try to outpace Blitzking: You can’t. He has double actions. You outthink him, not outspeed him.
  • ❌ Deploy only Pawns: Pawn builds are too slow. You need pieces that create immediate threats.

Blitzking vs Other Bosses

Blitzking is the only boss that wins on tempo rather than board control or raw power. This means strategies that work against other bosses often fail against Blitzking:

StrategyWorks AgainstFails Against Blitzking Because
Slow Gambit stackingJester, Grand MasterToo slow — Blitzking overwhelms before stack is ready
Economy farmingQueen of HeartsStock doesn’t help when you’re being checkmated
Pawn promotionKing of SpadesPawns get captured before they promote

Against Blitzking, tempo is king. Play fast, create threats, force responses.

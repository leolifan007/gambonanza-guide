Strategy Guide — Rated Builds & Proven Tactics

⚠ THE GAMBONANZA MINDSET
This is NOT chess. It's a deckbuilding roguelike that pretends to be chess. If you approach it like a chess game, you lose. If you approach it like Slay the Spire with pieces, you win. This guide is built on that principle.

Opening Builds — Ranked & Explained

Three proven openers. Pick one per run and commit.

Build #1: Knight Aggro S

S Highest win rate opening. Aggressive knight development into early checkmate threats.
Step Move Why
1 Develop both knights to center Knights on d4/e4 dominate 4×4 and 6×6 boards
2 Pawn push to clear diagonal Opens bishop + clears knight’s second L-jump
3 Knight+Bishop Combo Gambit Maximum reach — threaten 6+ squares simultaneously
4 Backstab on exposed piece Kill the defender, ignore protection
5 Checkmate with knight+queen Finish before opponent’s board develops
EARLY GAME Strongest turns 1-8. Falls off if game goes past move 15.

Best against: Non-boss boards, Rook Rook, Bishop Bluku
Weak against: Queen Gambit (she eats aggressive knights), King of Spades

Only 10h+ players know: On 4×4 boards, Knight Aggro can win in 4 moves against the AI. Open d4 knight → e4 knight → Backstab on f-pawn → checkmate. The AI literally cannot respond in time on boards smaller than 6×6. This is your speedrun opener.

Build #2: Pawn Wall Defense A

A Most consistent build. Survive early, dominate late. Lower win rate but higher survival.
Step Move Why
1 Push center pawns (d4, e4) Control the only squares that matter
2 Develop bishops behind pawns Ranged support while pawns block
3 Castle if possible King safety → economy protection
4 Sacrifice for Strength Turn pawn wall into mobility buff
5 Promote pawn → Queen with Heal Board Endgame conversion
MID GAME LATE GAME Weak early, unstoppable late. Needs patience.

Best against: Boss fights (survives longer), Queen Gambit
Weak against: Aggressive AIs that punish slow development

Only 10h+ players know: Pawn Wall + King's Shield creates an exploit where the AI wastes ALL its Gambits trying to break through. The AI prioritizes your king, but when it's shielded AND walled, it will burn 5+ Gambits on futile attacks while your bishops destroy everything from behind the wall.

Build #3: Economy Rush B

B High-risk build. Prioritize stock over board position. Dominate through purchases.
Step Move Why
1 Play safe, minimize piece losses Every lost piece = less stock
2 Double Down at 50+ stock Compound your first big win
3 Steal Investment from rich opponent Economy theft is faster than earning
4 Buy S-tier Gambits in bulk Out-gear the opponent
5 Win through raw card advantage 10 Gambits vs their 3 = auto-win
EARLY LATE Risky in mid-game because you're under-equipped. If you survive, you snowball.

Best against: Boss prep runs, long campaigns
Weak against: Early aggressive AIs that don’t let you farm

Only 10h+ players know: The Pawn Lottery expected value peaks at exactly 37 stock input. Input less and you lose to variance; input more and the returns diminish. Always bet exactly 37 stock in the Pawn Lottery for mathematically optimal long-term returns.

Advanced Gambit Combos

Combo #1: The Assassin S

Cards: Backstab + Chain Capture + King’s Shield

  1. Shield a knight with King’s Shield
  2. Backstab the enemy’s most protected piece
  3. Chain Capture triggers on the piece behind it
  4. Knight is invulnerable — no retaliation

Win condition: 2 captures in 1 move, 0 losses

Combo #2: The Reposition A

Cards: Teleport + Through the Ranks

  1. Teleport rook to open file
  2. Through the Ranks across 4+ squares
  3. Triggers Gambit effects + tile effects at each square
  4. Results in massive board swing

Win condition: One move generates 5+ turn value

Combo #3: The Comeback A

Cards: Sacrifice for Strength + Heal Board + Knight+Bishop Combo

  1. Sacrifice 2 pieces → all remaining pieces +1 range
  2. Use Knight+Bishop Combo on buffed pieces for 2× reach
  3. Heal Board to return sacrificed pieces
  4. Returned pieces benefit from the buff

Win condition: Buffed army with full roster

Combo #4: The Boss Killer S

Cards: Jackpot + specific Boss Counter + The Ultimate Counter

  1. Jackpot Gambit before boss fight → free counter Gambit
  2. Equip counter → neutralize boss mechanic
  3. The Ultimate Counter for Phase 3
  4. Boss has zero effective mechanics

Win condition: Boss becomes a regular AI opponent

Deck Composition Math

Deck Size Win Rate* Play Style Recommended For
12-18 cards ~70% Hyper-aggressive Speedruns, 4×4 boards, experienced players
19-25 cards ~75% Balanced Most players, most runs — this is the sweet spot
26-35 cards ~60% Control/late game Boss runs, new players learning
36+ cards ~45% Unfocused Avoid — dilution kills win rates

*Community-estimated. A focused 15-card deck of S/A-tier Gambits beats a 35-card deck with mixed tiers every time.

❌ THE GOLDEN RULE OF DECK SIZE
Every Gambit after #22 reduces your chance of drawing your best 5 Gambits. A bloated deck isn't versatile — it's diluted. Keep it under 22 cards unless your build specifically requires more.

Reading the Board — Phase by Phase

Turn 1-8: Development

Focus: Get 3+ pieces into active positions

  • Knights first, then bishops, pawns for coverage
  • DON’T bring out queen before turn 5
  • Count enemy Gambits used — they burn them faster than you think
  • If you’re behind after 8 turns, the board was wrong for your build

Turn 9-20: Execution

Focus: Coordinated attacks, Gambit chains

  • Look for 2-in-1 captures (Backstab + Chain)
  • If ahead: simplify. Trade pieces 1:1.
  • If behind: create chaos. Flip board with Teleport.
  • This is where most games are won or lost

Turn 21+: Endgame

Focus: Checkmate or survive

  • Push pawns to promotion — it’s your best path to victory
  • King activity matters now. Move king toward the action.
  • If winning: ladder mate with rooks
  • If losing: stall, look for comeback Gambit

Boss Fights: Special

Focus: Pattern recognition over aggression

  • Scout first move (throwaway piece)
  • Track boss Gambit cooldowns
  • Use boss counters on the specific turns they matter
  • Survive > Kill fast. Bosses have limited Gambit pools and WILL run out.

Tilt Management

🚫 THE 3-LOSS RULE
If you lose 3 runs in a row, STOP. Your decision-making is compromised. Take 10 minutes, review one replay, identify exactly which Gambit you should have bought instead. Come back fresh.

Common tilt mistakes:

  • Buying expensive Gambits out of frustration
  • Rushing into boss fights under-prepared
  • Ignoring shop rerolls when your build is stuck
  • Playing the same opener after it’s clearly not working

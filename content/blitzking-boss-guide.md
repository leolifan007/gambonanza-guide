## Blitzking: The Boss That Punishes Slow Play

<div class="callout callout-verdict">
  <strong>THE SHORT VERSION</strong><br>
  Blitzking wins by outpacing you. Every turn you spend "setting up," Blitzking takes 2 actions. The solution isn't to play faster — it's to play <strong>fewer, more impactful turns</strong>. This guide covers the exact piece loadout, Gambit picks, and turn-by-turn strategy I use to beat Blitzking consistently.
</div>

Blitzking is the third boss in Gambonanza, and the one that ends more runs than any other. The mechanic is simple: Blitzking gets an extra action every turn. That means while you move once, Blitzking moves twice. The math looks brutal — and it is, if you play Blitzking like a normal boss.

But Blitzking has a critical weakness: **speed without direction is chaos**. Blitzking's double moves are scripted to prioritize aggression, not efficiency. If you force Blitzking into a defensive position, those extra moves become wasted moves.

<div class="meta-rating">
  <span class="meta-badge meta-a">A</span>
  <span class="meta-label">Blitzking is beatable once you understand the pattern. My win rate went from 20% to 75% after I stopped trying to outpace him and started forcing him to waste turns.</span>
</div>

## Blitzking's Mechanics

### Core Ability: Double Action

Every turn, Blitzking:
1. Moves a piece (usually the most aggressive option)
2. Moves a second piece OR activates a Gambit

This means Blitzking gains material advantage faster than any other boss. By turn 5, Blitzking typically has +3 piece advantage if you haven't slowed him down.

### Blitzking's Gambit Pool

Blitzking draws from a specific set of Gambits:

| Gambit | Frequency | Effect | Counter |
|--------|-----------|--------|---------|
| Speed Boost | Very High | +1 additional move (3 total!) | Freeze Gambit |
| Double Move | High | Two moves in one action | Board Shrink (limits space) |
| Knight Rush | Medium | Deploys Knight to center | Early Check (forces Knight to block) |
| Pawn Wall | Medium | Spawns 2 Pawns as shields | Rook file clear |
| Emergency Teleport | Low | Teleports King to safety | Predict landing squares |

<div class="callout callout-tip">
  <strong>Pro Tip</strong><br>
  Blitzking's Gambit pool is <strong>predictable</strong>. Speed Boost and Double Move account for ~70% of his Gambit usage. If you prepare for those two, you're prepared for most of the fight.
</div>

### Blitzking's Weakness

Blitzking's AI prioritizes **immediate threats** over **long-term position**. This means:

- If you create a check threat, Blitzking will always address it immediately (even if the "correct" move is to ignore it)
- Blitzking never sacrifices a piece for positional advantage
- Blitzking never plays defensive Gambits unless forced

This is exploitable. You can waste Blitzking's turns by creating false threats.

## Recommended Piece Loadout

### Best Pieces (In Order)

| Piece | Why Against Blitzking |
|-------|----------------------|
| Knight | Knight forks force Blitzking to respond to 2 threats at once — wastes his extra action |
| Rook | Rook file control limits Blitzking's movement options across the whole board |
| Queen | Only if you have Teleport or Double Move. Naked Queen gets overwhelmed. |
| Bishop | Decent diagonal control but slower than Knight for creating threats |

### Pieces to Avoid

- **Pawn-heavy builds**: Pawns are too slow against Blitzking. By the time they promote, the game is over.
- **King-centric builds**: Your King is vulnerable. Blitzking will exploit any exposed King position within 2 turns.

## Best Gambits Against Blitzking

### Tier S — Mandatory Picks

| Gambit | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Freeze | Stops one of Blitzking's double actions. Effectively makes it your turn + 1 Blitzking turn instead of +2. |
| Early Check | Forces Blitzking to waste a turn on defense. Every forced defensive move is a turn Blitzking isn't attacking. |

### Tier A — Strong Picks

| Gambit | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Board Shrink | Less space = less value from Blitzking's extra moves. On a 4×4 board, 3 moves isn't much better than 2. |
| Swap | Swap a key Blitzking piece out of position. Wastes his setup turns. |
| Teleport | Reposition instantly. Don't spend turns walking when you can teleport. |

### Tier B — Situational

| Gambit | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Heal Board | Only useful if Blitzking is using Crumble heavily (he does ~30% of the time) |
| Defensive Shield | Keeps a key piece alive one more turn. Good but passive. |
| Pawn Rush | Desperate measure. Can overwhelm Blitzking's back rank if you're already ahead. |

## Turn-by-Turn Strategy

### Phase 1: Setup (Turns 1-3)

**Goal**: Create one credible threat without overcommitting.

```
Turn 1: Deploy Knight to center. Blitzking will attack with 2 moves, but Knight is hard to dislodge from center.
Turn 2: Develop a second piece (Bishop or Rook). Don't attack yet.
Turn 3: Play Freeze or Early Check if you have it. Force Blitzking to spend his extra actions on defense.
```

<div class="pro-tip">
  <strong>Only 10h+ players know:</strong> The most important turn against Blitzking is turn 3. If you can force Blitzking into a defensive position by turn 3, his double-action advantage becomes a liability — he's making 2 defensive moves instead of 2 offensive moves.
</div>

### Phase 2: Pressure (Turns 4-6)

**Goal**: Convert your early threat into a material advantage.

```
Turn 4: Knight fork if possible. Blitzking must address 2 threats.
Turn 5: If Blitzking is defending, press harder. Deploy Queen if you haven't.
Turn 6: Check the King. Even if it's not mate, forcing Blitzking to move his King wastes 1-2 of his actions.
```

### Phase 3: Close (Turns 7+)

**Goal**: Checkmate or insurmountable material advantage.

```
Turn 7+: Keep checking. Blitzking's AI can't handle sustained pressure.
         Every check = 1 guaranteed wasted Blitzking action.
         Two checks per turn = Blitzking uses all actions on defense.
```

## The Wasted-Turn Trap

The key insight for Blitzking: **make him waste turns**.

<div class="synergy-table" style="overflow-x:auto">
  <table>
    <thead><tr><th>Your Action</th><th>Blitzking's Response</th><th>Net Result</th></tr></thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr><td>Early Check</td><td>Must block (1 action)</td><td>Blitzking loses 1 of 2 actions</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Knight Fork</td><td>Must save one piece (1-2 actions)</td><td>Blitzking may lose both actions</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Freeze</td><td>Loses 1 action entirely</td><td>Equal turns — your advantage</td></tr>
      <tr><td>Check + Fork</td><td>Must address check first, fork still active</td><td>Blitzking can't save both</td></tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

If you can consistently waste 1+ of Blitzking's actions per turn, the double-action mechanic is neutralized. From that point, it's a normal chess game — and you should be winning normal chess games by the time you reach Blitzking.

## What NOT to Do Against Blitzking

- ❌ **Play slow setups**: Every setup turn gives Blitzking 2 actions. That's 2 free attacks.
- ❌ **Ignore the King**: Blitzking will checkmate you in 4 turns if your King is exposed. Guard the back rank.
- ❌ **Save Gambits for "the right moment"**: Against Blitzking, the right moment is now. Use Freeze on turn 1 if it gives you tempo.
- ❌ **Try to outpace Blitzking**: You can't. He has double actions. You outthink him, not outspeed him.
- ❌ **Deploy only Pawns**: Pawn builds are too slow. You need pieces that create immediate threats.

## Blitzking vs Other Bosses

Blitzking is the only boss that wins on <strong>tempo</strong> rather than <strong>board control</strong> or <strong>raw power</strong>. This means strategies that work against other bosses often fail against Blitzking:

| Strategy | Works Against | Fails Against Blitzking Because |
|----------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| Slow Gambit stacking | Jester, Grand Master | Too slow — Blitzking overwhelms before stack is ready |
| Economy farming | Queen of Hearts | Stock doesn't help when you're being checkmated |
| Pawn promotion | King of Spades | Pawns get captured before they promote |

Against Blitzking, <strong>tempo is king</strong>. Play fast, create threats, force responses.

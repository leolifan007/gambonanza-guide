# How to Win from 0 Stock - Emergency Recovery Guide

## Quick Fix

**You CAN win from 0 stock. Here's the 3-step recovery:**

<div class="synergy-table" style="overflow-x:auto">

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Stop spending | Every stock point matters |
| 2 | Check opponent's board | Find their weakness |
| 3 | Find free stock sources | Some Gambits/Pieces generate stock |
| 4 | Turtle until turn 8 | Comeback window opens late |

</div>

**Free stock sources:**
- Pawn moves with "Pawn Parade" Gambit: +1 stock per move
- "Second Wind" Gambit: +20 stock when below 10
- Selling Pieces: 50% of original cost

---

## Step 1: Stop Spending (Immediately)

**What to do:**
- Don't buy any pieces
- Don't use any Gambits that cost stock
- Move pieces to generate value (Pawns with Pawn Parade)

**Free stock sources:**
<div class="synergy-table" style="overflow-x:auto">

| Source | Stock Generated |
|--------|-----------------|
| Pawn Parade Gambit | +1 per Pawn move |
| Second Wind Gambit | +20 when below 10 stock |
| Selling pieces | 50% of original cost |

</div>

## Step 2: Check Opponent's Weakness

**What to look for:**
- Overextended pieces (you can capture)
- Exposed King (you can attack)
- Stock lead but weak board position

**Decision tree:**
<div class="synergy-table" style="overflow-x:auto">

| Opponent State | Your Best Move |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Overextended | Counterattack |
| Turtle mode | Build economy |
| Strong position | Wait for mistake |

</div>

## Step 3: The Comeback Window

**When to attack:**
- You have 20+ stock
- Opponent made a mistake (overextended, exposed King)
- You have a combo ready

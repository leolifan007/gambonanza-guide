## Boss Battle Guide

Every boss in Gambonanza has unique mechanics that break standard chess rules. Learning each boss's pattern is the difference between repeated losses and satisfying victories.

---

## Boss Overview

Gambonanza features 8 unique bosses, each representing a different piece type from chess. Each boss has special abilities tied to their piece type and gets progressively harder.

## Boss 1: Rook Rook

**Piece Type**: Rook | **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐ (Easy)

### The Fight
Rook Rook can move twice per turn and attacks along ranks and files with devastating precision. However, rooks are fundamentally limited — they can't move diagonally, making them predictable.

### Strategy
- **Control the center** — rooks dominate open lines but struggle in blocked positions
- **Use diagonal Gambits** — bishop-type movement Gambits can flank where rooks can't reach
- **Never let a rook reach the 7th rank** — that's where rooks are most devastating
- **Trade pieces early** — rooks without support are vulnerable

### Recommended Gambits
- Knight+Bishop Combo (flexible flanking)
- Through the Ranks (counter their rank attack)
- Pierce Through (break their defense line)

## Boss 2: Bishop Bluku

**Piece Type**: Bishop | **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

### The Fight
Bishop Bluku controls both diagonals simultaneously and can swap squares with any piece on the same diagonal. They create crossfire positions that are deceptively hard to break.

### Strategy
- **Control the light/dark squares** — bishops are color-bound; if you control both colors, you limit their diagonal control
- **Create blocked positions** — pawn chains block bishops completely
- **Use knight movement** — knights attack squares bishops can't reach
- **Avoid diagonal exchanges** — bishops are excellent in trades; avoid giving them what they want

### Recommended Gambits
- Knight+Bishop Combo (covers both angles)
- Surround (trap bishops in blocked positions)
- Bishop's Blind (removes diagonal control temporarily)

---

## Boss 3: Knightmare

**Piece Type**: Knight | **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

### The Fight
Knightmare moves in unpredictable L-shapes and can jump over pieces. They create forked positions constantly — one move threatening two critical targets.

### Strategy
- **Control L-shaped squares** — knights attack in L patterns; if you occupy those squares, you limit their moves
- **Don't cluster pieces** — knight forks are most dangerous when pieces are grouped together
- **Use teleport Gambits** — instant repositioning counters knight unpredictability
- **Trade at the right moment** — knights are strongest in the middle of the board

### Knight Fork Counters
- Knight's Parry Gambit (blocks the fork)
- Undo Move (recover from a fork)
- King's Shield (protect the king from being forked)

## Boss 4: Queen Gambit

**Piece Type**: Queen | **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Hard)

### The Fight
Queen Gambit is the most dangerous regular boss. She combines rook and bishop movement, threatens massive swaths of the board, and has a special promotion ability — she can promote pawns instantly.

### Strategy
- **Exchange her pieces** — keeping queens off the board is priority #1
- **Control open lines** — queens are weakest in closed positions
- **Watch the promotion ability** — she can instantly create multiple queens or powerful pieces
- **Use queen-specific counters** — Queen's Gambit Counter is essential here

### Critical Gambits
- **Queen's Gambit Counter** (blocks promotion ability)
- Teleport (escape queen threats quickly)
- Chain Capture (multi-capture disrupts queen combinations)

### Warning
Queen Gambit gets +50% attack power if she reaches the center of the board. Control e4/d4/e5/d5 at all costs.

## Boss 5: King of Spades

**Piece Type**: King | **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very Hard)

### The Fight
The King of Spades is the hardest boss in Gambonanza. He can move to any adjacent square and trigger a special ability — teleport any of your pieces off the board. He's both mobile AND disruptive.

### Strategy
- **Keep the king contained** — every square he reaches is a piece he can remove
- **Use king's shield constantly** — protect your most valuable pieces from his teleport ability
- **Avoid leaving pieces adjacent to him** — his movement range means he can reach most squares quickly
- **Build for attrition** — this fight is a war of attrition, not a quick checkmate

### Boss Gambit Required
- **King's Counter** — without this, the fight is extremely difficult
- This Gambit blocks his teleport ability for 3 turns

### Phase 2
At 50% health, King of Spades enters Phase 2 and gains the ability to place traps on squares. Bait him into wasting moves on trap placement.

## Boss 6: Pawn Storm

**Piece Type**: Pawns | **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐ (Easy but Different)

### The Fight
Not a single boss but a swarm. Pawn Storm throws 16 pawns at you at once, overwhelming your position through sheer numbers. They move fast and in formation.

### Strategy
- **Don't try to capture them all** — that's a trap. Focus on the advance pawns
- **Create chokepoints** — force pawns into narrow spaces where their movement is limited
- **Attack from the sides** — pawns can't move diagonally, so flanking attacks are devastating
- **Control promotion squares** — if even one pawn promotes, it becomes a serious threat

### Recommended Gambits
- Surround (swarm containment)
- Backstab (attack from behind advancing pawns)
- Gravity Flip (invert their advance direction)

---

## Boss 7: Castle Master

**Piece Type**: Castling | **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium-Hard)

### The Fight
Castle Master specializes in castling — they can move both king and rook simultaneously to create fortified defensive positions. Breaking through their castle is the challenge.

### Strategy
- **Force them to move early** — castling is a setup move; if you pressure them before they can castle, they waste a turn
- **Attack the king directly** — once castled, the king is safer but the rook is less active
- **Use long-range Gambits** — bishops and queens can probe through castle walls
- **Don't rush the castle** — patience wins here; squeeze them positionally

### Recommended Gambits
- Rook's Wall (block their castling setup)
- Pierce Through (attack through castle walls)
- Through the Ranks (long-range pressure)

## Boss 8: The Grandmaster (Final Boss)

**Piece Type**: All Pieces | **Difficulty**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Extreme)

### The Fight
The Grandmaster uses all piece types, has all abilities, and gets progressively harder each time you deal damage. This is the ultimate test of everything you've learned.

### Strategy
- **You need a full toolkit** — no single Gambit wins this fight
- **Phase 1 (100-66% HP)**: Learn their pattern. They open with a standard opening.
- **Phase 2 (66-33% HP)**: They start using Boss Gambits. Counter with your own.
- **Phase 3 (33-0% HP)**: They play perfectly. Speed is everything — don't give them time to think.

### Ultimate Gambit Strategy
- **The Ultimate Counter** — use this in Phase 3 when every move counts
- **Jackpot Gambit** — farm this before the fight for boss-specific counters
- **Heal Board** — you'll take damage in Phases 1 and 2; save healing for Phase 3

### Pre-Fight Preparation
Before attempting The Grandmaster:
1. Unlock 8+ Boss Gambits
2. Reach +50% stock value for economic buffer
3. Have at least 2 teleport Gambits
4. Practice Phase 3 without healing to understand the speed required

## General Boss Tips

### Preparation Before Any Boss
- Visit the shop and buy boss-specific counters
- Use Jackpot Gambit to unlock special boss abilities
- Build economy — you'll want a stock buffer in case you lose

### During the Fight
- **First move matters** — most bosses have a predictable opening; exploit it
- **Watch for patterns** — bosses repeat patterns; learn them fast
- **Don't panic Gambit** — sometimes the obvious Gambit isn't the right one

### After Losing
- Review which Gambit would have changed the outcome
- Buy that Gambit in the shop
- Return better prepared

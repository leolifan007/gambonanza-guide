This Isn’t a Quick Tutorial — It’s a Clear Roadmap

Gambonanza blends chess and roguelike mechanics beautifully, but the learning curve is steep. This guide won’t drill into micro-tactics — it tells you what to do and when.

Verdict
Beginners make exactly 2 mistakes consistently: over-expanding in the early game, and failing to transition in mid-game. Fix those two things and beating Grand Master is just a matter of time.

Phase 1: Opening (3×3 → 4×4)

Goal

Build an economy base + fill Gambit slots.

Action Checklist

  • Use a recommended seed: 1238513920 or 44488822
  • Prioritize economy Gambits (Pawn Gambit, Gold Rush)
  • Keep your King near the center with at least 2 defensive pieces
  • Don’t expose your Queen too early
Golden Opening Formula
T1-3: Expansion ×2 + Gambit ×1 | T4-6: Economy ×2 + Defense ×1
Follow this ratio and you'll never "crash" in the opening phase.

Mid-game — note the piece positions and board control strategy

Phase 2: Mid-Game (4×4 → 5×5)

Goal

Transition to combat configuration + prepare for the first boss.

Key Decision Tree

` First boss is Jester? ├─ Yes → Keep expanding, fast kill on 4×4 └─ No → Switch to defensive formation, fill Gambit slots

8+ pieces on the board already? ├─ Yes → Start clearing low-level pieces, keep high-value ones └─ No → Continue expanding but control the pace `

Common Traps

  • Mid Over-expanding: Deployed 10+ pieces on 4×4, but can’t afford upgrades — this is the #1 death cause
  • Ignoring Gambits: If you haven’t upgraded Gambit slots by mid-game, 5×5 bosses will crush you
  • Hoarding pieces: Low-level Pawns after 4×4 are just dead weight. Sell them

Phase 3: Boss Phase (5×5)

Boss Quick Reference

See the Full Boss Guide for detail. Key points:

Boss Mentality
Lost to a boss 3 times in a row? Stop bashing your head. Check: is your Gambit loadout correct? Are your relics the right type? Swapping one Gambit often works better than changing 10 pieces.

Phase 4: Endgame (6×6+)

Transition to Endgame Mode

Sell all economy relics → Open all combat relics → Start hunting for King kill windows

Win Rate Checkpoints

Before entering 6×6, confirm these conditions:

ConditionRequirement
Gambit SlotsAt least 2, ideally 3
Combat Relics≥ 4
King PositionNot exposed, has defense
Core PiecesQueen + Rook ×2 + Bishop ×2

If you don’t meet these, lower expectations and pivot to a King hunt strategy instead of a direct battle.

5 Beginner Traps to Avoid

  1. More pieces = better → No, quality trumps quantity
  2. Saving Gambits → Gambits are meant to be used, not hoarded
  3. Skipping relic shops → First thing at every new board: check the relic shop
  4. Pawn promotion = always good → Losing Pawn Gambit synergy can cost more than the promotion gain
  5. Panicking when King is exposed → You have 3 turns to reinforce. Breath. Fix the defense.

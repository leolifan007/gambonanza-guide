Why 4x4 Boards Are Your ATM

THE SHORT VERSION

The smallest board in Gambonanza = fastest wins per minute. If your stock is below 50, stop doing 6x6 and drop down to 4x4. This isn’t about winning pretty, it’s about winning cheap.

Knight Aggro Opener (Fastest Start)

The opening that beats 90% of 4x4 games in 4 turns:

Turn 1: Move Knight to center (attacks 2 pieces)
Turn 2: Knight captures (or threatens capture)
Turn 3: Bring second piece forward
Turn 4: Checkmate or forced promotion

Why It Works on 4x4

  • 4x4 boards have no room to retreat
  • A Knight in the center attacks 2-3 pieces from turn 1
  • Opponent can’t develop without losing a piece
  • Games end before economy gambits even matter

Pro Tip

On 4x4, don’t bother with economy gambits in the first 2 turns. The match is too short to recoup the cost. Focus on attack gambits that end the game fast.

Farm Loop Timing

The optimal farm cycle:

  1. Match 1-3: Play Knight Aggro. Ignore economy. Build muscle memory.
  2. Match 4-6: Introduce 1-2 economy gambits (Double Down, Stock Split)
  3. Match 7+: Full optimization-attack openers into economy finish

Only 10h+ players know:
Stock resets aren’t punishing on 4x4. If you lose a match, you’re back in the game within 3 minutes. Don’t get tilted-just queue the next 4x4 and go again.

What NOT to Do on 4x4

  • Don’t play defensive gambits (no room to retreat anyway)
  • Don’t invest in long-term economy gambits (match too short)
  • Don’t play “fair” chess-the board is rigged for aggression
  • Don’t promote pawns (wastes 1-2 turns you don’t have)

Speedrun Records

From my own runs, here are the benchmarks I’ve hit on the 4x4 board:

  • Fastest 4x4 win (no gambits): 6 moves
  • Fastest 4x4 with economy farming: 4 moves + 2 turns
  • Highest stock/min record: ~22 stock/min (still trying to beat this)

When to Stop Farming 4x4

Move up to 6x6 when:

  • You have stock >200
  • You’re farming for boss prep (need board space)
  • You want to test actual strategy
  • 4x4 feels too easy (you’ll know when)

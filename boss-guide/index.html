Boss Overview

Based on community data from GameBrief’s boss guide, YouTube first-clear videos, and the Gambonanza Wiki:

Total bosses: 8 stage bosses + 1 Grandmaster final boss

Boss frequency: One boss every 2 stages (Stages 2, 4, 6, 8, 10)

Difficulty curve: Each boss is harder than the last, with a significant spike at Stage 6+

Source: GameBrief breaks down every boss fight and what to expect at each stage (source).

Early Bosses (Stages 2-4)

Boss 1 - Stage 2

Difficulty: Easy

The first boss is designed to be beatable even with a weak build. It has limited mechanics and simple AI patterns.

Strategy:

  • No special preparation needed
  • Basic piece positioning is enough
  • Save your Ultimate Counter for later bosses

Boss 2 - Stage 4

Difficulty: Moderate

Introduces basic phase transitions. The boss changes behavior at 50% HP.

Strategy:

  • Have at least one defensive gambit ready
  • Don’t overextend in Phase 1 - save pieces for Phase 2
  • Heal Board is useful here if you have it

Mid Bosses (Stages 6-8)

Boss 3 - Stage 6

Difficulty: Hard

The first real wall for new players. This boss has aggressive piece targeting and punishes overextension.

Strategy from community:

  • Phase 1: Establish board control without overcommitting
  • Phase 2: Boss gets aggressive. Use Teleport to reposition
  • Keep stock reserves for emergency gambit purchases

Boss 4 - Stage 8

Difficulty: Very Hard

This is where King difficulty runs commonly end. The boss has multi-phase mechanics and can swing a winning position into a loss in one turn.

Strategy:

  • Save The Ultimate Counter for Phase 3
  • Have at least 20 stock entering the fight
  • Prioritize piece survival over damage

The Grandmaster (Stage 10 Final Boss)

Difficulty: Extreme

The Grandmaster is Gambonanza’s ultimate challenge. Per GameBrief: “The Grandmaster closes every run as the stage 5 final boss. It’s a multi-phase fight; players report distinct phase transitions with changed behavior” (source).

Phase breakdown:

Phase 1: Grandmaster Standard

  • Normal AI with enhanced stats
  • Standard piece placement
  • Goal: Establish position without losing pieces

Phase 2: Grandmaster Aggressive

  • AI becomes aggressive, targets key pieces
  • Increased capture frequency
  • Goal: Survive. Don’t try to win here. Just don’t lose.

Phase 3: Grandmaster Desperation

  • Multiple threats per turn
  • AI sacrifices pieces for position
  • Goal: Use The Ultimate Counter here. This is the phase where most runs end.

True Final Boss (King difficulty Stage 6): Some King difficulty runs have reported a Stage 6 with a “True Final Boss” beyond the standard Grandmaster. YouTube footage shows this extended encounter adds unique mechanics (source).

Pre-Boss Checklist

Before every boss fight:

  • Do I have at least 15 stock?
  • Do I have a defensive gambit ready?
  • Is my board position solid?
  • Do I know when to use my Ultimate Counter?
  • Can I survive Phase 1 without losing pieces?
  • What’s my win condition in Phase 3?
